Understanding Eye Conditions

Myopia (Nearsightedness)

Difficulty seeing distant objects clearly. Caused by an elongated eyeball or excessive curvature of the cornea.

Hypermetropia (Farsightedness)

Difficulty focusing on close objects. Happens when the eyeball is too short or the cornea has too little curvature.

Presbyopia (Age-related Vision Loss)

Loss of near focusing ability due to aging, usually after 40 years old. Related to hardening of the lens inside the eye.
